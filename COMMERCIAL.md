ExtensionShield’s **core** is MIT-licensed. That means you’re free to use it commercially, including in proprietary products and services.

If you’re building something on top of ExtensionShield (SaaS, an internal security tool, consulting, or resale), we’d genuinely love to hear from you — not to restrict you, but to learn what you’re building and how we can make ExtensionShield better.

Reaching out is **optional** and **non-binding** — it is **not a condition of the MIT License**. This is simply a friendly request and does not create any obligation beyond the MIT License.

If ExtensionShield helps you, consider giving back in any way that fits:
- Share feedback or a quick note about your use case
- Contribute a PR or documentation improvement
- Sponsor the project (even **$1** helps keep the scanner maintained)
- Support our community work — we run open-source programs and fund internships when we can
